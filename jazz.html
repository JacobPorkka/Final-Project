Jazz

Jazz music is a vibrant and improvisational genre that emerged in the late 19th and early 20th centuries in the United States, particularly in African American communities. Characterized by its syncopated rhythms, expressive melodies, and intricate harmonies, jazz encompasses a wide range of styles, including swing, bebop, cool jazz, and fusion. At its core, jazz is about creativity, spontaneity, and individual expression, with musicians often engaging in lively improvisation during performances. It has a rich history of innovation and influence, shaping and being shaped by cultural, social, and political movements throughout the 20th century and beyond.
