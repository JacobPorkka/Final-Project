Country

Country music is a genre rooted in the folk music traditions of rural America, particularly the southern United States. It often features storytelling lyrics accompanied by acoustic instruments such as guitar, fiddle, banjo, and harmonica. Themes commonly found in country music include love, heartbreak, family, and the rural way of life. The genre encompasses a wide range of styles, from traditional country and bluegrass to more contemporary subgenres like country pop and country rock. Country music has a rich history dating back to the early 20th century, and it has evolved over time to incorporate influences from other genres such as rock, pop, and blues. It remains a popular genre both in the United States and internationally, with artists like Johnny Cash, Dolly Parton, and Garth Brooks achieving legendary status within the genre.
