Hip-Hop

Hip-hop music is a genre that originated in the Bronx, New York City, in the 1970s, characterized by its rhythmic spoken lyrics, often accompanied by beats created by sampling or synthesizing other tracks. It emerged as a cultural movement, encompassing not only music but also elements such as graffiti art, breakdancing, and DJing. Hip-hop lyrics often reflect the experiences and struggles of urban life, addressing themes such as social inequality, street life, and personal expression. The genre has evolved over the years, branching into various subgenres such as gangsta rap, conscious rap, and trap music, each with its own distinct sound and themes.
