Soul Music

Soul music, born from the depths of African-American culture in the United States during the 1950s and 1960s, is a genre that speaks to the heart and spirit. Characterized by its emotive vocals, fervent rhythms, and expressive instrumentation, soul music emerged as a powerful form of self-expression and cultural identity. Rooted in gospel, rhythm and blues, and jazz, soul music encompasses themes of love, resilience, and social justice, serving as a soundtrack to both personal triumphs and societal struggles. With legendary artists like Aretha Franklin, Marvin Gaye, and Otis Redding at its forefront, soul music continues to resonate with audiences worldwide, reminding us of the enduring power of the human soul and the universal language of music.

These are the top 10 Soul Music songs said by Timeout

  • ‘Let’s Stay Together’ – Al Green
  • ‘I Want You’ – Marvin Gaye
  • ‘I Heard it Through the Grapevine’ – Marvin Gaye
  • ‘The Tracks of My Tears’ – Smokey Robinson
  • ‘Get Ready’ – The Temptations
  • ‘Soul Man’ – Sam & Dave
  • ‘Try A Little Tenderness’ – Otis Redding
  • ‘Living for the City’ – Stevie Wonder
  • Move On Up’ – Curtis Mayfield
